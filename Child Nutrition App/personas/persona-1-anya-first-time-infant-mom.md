### Background

Anya is a first-time mom still on maternity leave from her job at a small design studio in Jakarta. She gave birth to Bagas four months ago and describes herself as "obsessed with getting it right." She reads parenting content constantly—Instagram Reels, mom Facebook groups, and YouTube. Her mother-in-law gives a lot of unsolicited advice about traditional foods, which creates low-level tension.

She is aware that Bagas is approaching the complementary feeding window and feels a mix of excitement and anxiety. She has no older children to compare with and defaults to over-researching everything.

---

### Goals

1. Understand what nutrition Bagas will need once she starts solids at 6 months.
2. Have a clear, simple reference to track whether she's offering the right variety and amounts.
3. Feel less dependent on conflicting advice from her mother-in-law and random online sources.
4. Document Bagas's feeding progress so she can share it with his pediatrician.

---

### Pain Points

- **Information overload:** Too many sources, many conflicting. She doesn't know which ones to trust.
- **Fear of mistakes:** Anxious about introducing allergenic foods in the wrong order or missing key nutrients.
- **Ibu mertua pressure:** Her mother-in-law insists on traditional feeding norms (e.g., giving rice water at 3 months) that conflict with what Anya reads online.
- **No baseline:** She has no mental model of what "enough" looks like for a 6-month-old.
- **Milk mode confusion:** She currently only breastfeeds and doesn't know if there's anything to track yet.

---

### Motivations

- Wants to be the kind of mom who is informed and proactive, not reactive.
- Values science-backed guidance but presented in approachable, non-clinical language.
- Motivated by reassurance — she doesn't need perfection, she needs confidence.

---

### Behaviors

- Opens parenting content 3–5 times per day, often at night during feeding sessions.
- Saves recipes on Pinterest and sends them to herself on WhatsApp to "try later."
- Joins every Jakarta mom group she finds; lurks more than she posts.
- Tracks Bagas's weight informally using a home scale and a notes app.

---

### Relationship to Suppa

- **Entry point:** Discovers Suppa via an Instagram Reel or a recommendation in a Jakarta mom group.
- **Primary use case (M1):** Growth tracking and preparing for the 6-month transition; milk mode with a clear "coming soon" path to solids.
- **Key value:** A single, reassuring source that speaks her language and doesn't overwhelm her with clinical detail.
- **Risk:** If the app feels too complex or medical in tone, she'll bounce. She needs warmth and simplicity first.

### Research Interview Guide

**Goal:** Understand pre-MPASI anxiety, information sources, and readiness signals.

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Tell me about Bagas and what his first months have been like.
- How do you currently track his development or feeding?

**Core questions (20 min)**
1. Where do you go when you have a question about Bagas's nutrition? What do you trust most and why?
2. Walk me through the last time you felt worried about whether Bagas was getting enough. What did you do?
3. What does "enough nutrition" mean to you right now, while you're still exclusively breastfeeding?
4. What are you most anxious about as you approach the 6-month mark for starting solids?
5. If there were an app that tracked Bagas's nutrition — what would it need to show you to feel reassured?

**Usability probe (10 min)**
- Show the Today / milk-mode screen. Ask: "What does this tell you? What do you wish it said?"
- Show the Growth screen. Ask: "Would you use this? What would make you trust the numbers here?"

**Wrap-up**
- What would make you recommend an app like this to a friend in your mom group?
