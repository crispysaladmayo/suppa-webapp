## "Dua anak, dua kebutuhan berbeda. Aku yang harus ingat semuanya."
## *(Two kids, two different needs. I'm the one who has to remember it all.)*

### Background

Ratna is managing two completely different nutritional worlds simultaneously. Nadia just started complementary feeding (MPASI) at 6 months, so Ratna is back in the phase of introducing textures, tracking iron-rich first foods, and watching for reactions. Meanwhile, Rafi is a preschooler with established preferences — he eats well but is going through a phase of demanding the same meals repeatedly ("nasi goreng terus").

Ratna juggles freelance work from home while managing both kids. Her husband helps when he's around but doesn't engage with nutrition decisions. She is more experienced than a first-time mom but finds managing two separate sets of needs more mentally taxing than she expected. She's organized by nature and already uses a paper notebook to track Nadia's new foods — but it's scattered.

---

### Goals

1. Track Nadia's MPASI progression — which new foods have been introduced, any reactions noted, and what's next.
2. Know if Rafi is getting a nutritionally balanced week even when he's in a repetitive eating phase.
3. Plan meals that work for both children (or efficiently diverge where needed) to reduce cooking workload.
4. Have a single place where both children's nutritional contexts live — so she doesn't have to hold it all in her head.

---

### Pain Points

- **Dual-track mental load:** Two sets of age-appropriate foods, two sets of allergies (Rafi has none confirmed; Nadia untested), two sets of textures and preferences.
- **MPASI reintroduction tracking:** She introduced egg yolk with Nadia last week, but she can't remember exactly when or whether there was any reaction. She needs a log.
- **Rafi's repetitive eating:** She knows Rafi's diet is narrow lately but doesn't know if it's still "okay enough."
- **Time fragmentation:** Her work-from-home setup means she's interrupted constantly. She needs apps she can use in 2-minute windows.
- **No multi-child support in most apps:** The apps she's seen are child-centric (one child) and don't handle switching between two kids gracefully.

---

### Motivations

- Highly organized; derives satisfaction from having a system that works.
- Driven by the feeling of being "on top of things" — even if imperfectly.
- Wants the cognitive load transferred to an external system she can trust.
- Particularly motivated to nail Nadia's MPASI phase correctly — she felt she wasn't systematic enough with Rafi.

---

### Behaviors

- Uses WhatsApp heavily to coordinate with her sister and husband about childcare.
- Keeps a paper notebook for Nadia's MPASI log; admits it's messy and incomplete.
- Shops at the local market twice a week; supplements with Tokopedia for specific items.
- Meal preps on Sunday mornings; cooks simple, Batak-influenced food.
- Researches MPASI progression on YouTube and parenting forums at night.

---

### Relationship to Suppa

- **Entry point:** Parenting forum post, a Medan mom group, or YouTube MPASI content recommendation.
- **Primary use case (M1):** Growth tracking for both children; meal logging per child; Nadia's milk mode → MPASI progression; fridge-to-recipe for meals that work for the household.
- **Key value:** A child-switchable view so she doesn't have to exit and re-enter every time. Clear MPASI-appropriate recipe filtering for Nadia. Visual week patterns for both kids.
- **Risk:** If multi-child switching is clunky, she'll default to only tracking one and the app won't stick. She needs to feel the app was *designed* for her situation, not just tolerant of it.

### Research Interview Guide

**Goal:** Understand multi-child nutrition management, MPASI tracking needs, and the mental load of being the single nutrition decision-maker.

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Tell me about Rafi and Nadia — what are their personalities and relationships with food?
- Walk me through a recent meal that worked well for both of them (or didn't).

**Core questions (20 min)**
1. How do you currently keep track of what each child has eaten or tried this week?
2. For Nadia's MPASI — how do you decide what new food to introduce next? How do you record it?
3. Describe a moment where you felt overwhelmed by the nutritional responsibility for both kids at once.
4. If you could hand one piece of the nutrition tracking job to an app, what would it be?
5. What does "a good nutritional week" look like for Rafi? For Nadia? Are those different mental frameworks for you?

**Usability probe (10 min)**
- Show the household / child selection in onboarding. Ask: "How would you set up both kids? Does anything feel missing?"
- Show Today for Nadia (milk/MPASI mode). Ask: "Is this useful for where Nadia is right now? What would you want to add?"

**Wrap-up**
- If this app handled both children's tracking in one place — what's the one thing it would need to get right to earn your trust?
