## "I only have 20 minutes to decide what to cook. Don't make me think."

---

---

### Background

Dewi returned to work when Kira was 4 months old. She and her husband both have demanding schedules and rely on a part-time helper (asisten) who preps and feeds Kira during the day. Dewi handles morning and evening meals herself. She feels perpetual background guilt that she's not "doing enough" nutritionally, especially because she can't oversee every meal.

She is pragmatic and efficiency-driven. She doesn't want to read long articles — she wants a clear action: "what should I cook tonight that covers what Kira missed today?" She's already tried two other nutrition apps and abandoned them both within a week because they were too manual.

---

### Goals

1. Know at a glance what Kira ate during the day (via the asisten's input or her own quick logging) and what gaps remain for dinner.
2. Get a recipe suggestion in under 30 seconds based on what she actually has at home.
3. Build a small rotation of Kira-approved, nutritious weeknight meals that she can repeat without second-guessing.
4. Feel less guilty on days she can't cook — find reassurance that the week is still "okay overall."

---

### Pain Points

- **Time scarcity:** Logging meals feels like another chore on top of a full workday.
- **Delegation gap:** The asisten isn't tech-savvy and can't use apps; Dewi logs from memory at the end of the day, which feels inaccurate.
- **Decision fatigue at 6 PM:** After work, she has no mental energy to research what to cook.
- **Past app failures:** Previous apps required too much manual nutrient entry. She gave up.
- **Worry about hidden gaps:** She doesn't know if Kira is getting enough iron or vitamin D; she can't tell from behavior alone.

---

### Motivations

- Efficiency over depth — she'll trade clinical accuracy for instant, usable answers.
- Wants to feel like a "good enough" mom on the nutrition front without it consuming her evenings.
- Responds to visible progress: if she can see a "week looks okay" indicator, she relaxes.
- Trusts patterns over perfection — she wants to build repeatable habits, not track every gram.

---

### Behaviors

- Checks her phone during commute and lunch. Evenings are tight but she browses briefly after Kira sleeps.
- Uses GoPay/Tokopedia heavily; would consider in-app shopping integration eventually.
- Meal preps slightly on Sundays when she can — but only if she knows what to make.
- Sends food photos to her mom and husband on WhatsApp; occasionally asks "is this okay for Kira?"

---

### Relationship to Suppa

- **Entry point:** Tokopedia ad, or a colleague who's also a mom recommends it.
- **Primary use case (M1):** Quick daily log → Today gap view → fridge-based recipe suggestion for dinner.
- **Key value:** The fridge-to-recipe feature is the killer feature for her. If it works, she'll stay.
- **Risk:** Any screen that requires more than 3 taps to complete a primary action will cause drop-off. She needs a visible "week view" to feel the cumulative benefit without logging perfectly every day.

### Research Interview Guide

**Goal:** Understand time-constrained decision-making and what "enough" nutrition looks like for a busy working mom.

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Walk me through a typical Tuesday for you and Kira, from morning to bedtime.
- How do meals usually happen on a weekday?

**Core questions (20 min)**
1. How do you currently decide what to cook or order for Kira in the evenings?
2. Have you ever tried tracking what Kira eats? What happened?
3. What would make you actually log something on a day when you're exhausted?
4. When you feel worried about Kira's nutrition, what does that worry look and feel like?
5. If an app could only do one thing for you around Kira's nutrition — what would it be?

**Usability probe (10 min)**
- Show the Fridge screen. Ask: "Walk me through how you'd use this on a Wednesday evening."
- Show the Today screen (with partial day logged). Ask: "What does this tell you? What action would you take based on this?"

**Wrap-up**
- What would make you open this app every day vs only occasionally?
