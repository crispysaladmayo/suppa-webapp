# Persona 3 — Siti Nurhaliza
## "Zahra mau makan apa? Sami mawon — nolak semua."
## *(What does Zahra want to eat? Same as usual — she rejects everything.)*

---

---

### Background

Siti left teaching when Zahra was born and has been her full-time caregiver ever since. She genuinely enjoys cooking and follows Sundanese home cooking closely — lots of vegetables, tempeh, tofu, and rice. The problem is Zahra. At 2.5 years old, Zahra has entered an intensely picky eating phase: she refuses most vegetables, will only eat certain textures, and rejects anything new on sight.

Siti's mother (Zahra's nenek) tells her to "just give what the child eats" and not worry. But Siti has read enough to know that picky eating at this stage can create real nutrient gaps — especially protein, iron, and vegetables. She's tried hiding vegetables in meals, using fun shapes, and offering variety, but results are inconsistent. She doesn't know what Zahra is actually getting nutritionally on any given day.

---

### Goals

1. Find recipes that have a realistic chance of being accepted by a picky 2-year-old.
2. Know whether Zahra's "white food" days (just rice and crackers) are occasionally okay or cumulatively harmful.
3. Build a small library of family-tested, Zahra-approved meals she can rely on.
4. Feel less alone in dealing with picky eating — she wants validation that this is normal AND ideas for how to close gaps.

---

### Pain Points

- **Picky eating wall:** Zahra's rejection of new foods means Siti cooks the same 6 meals on rotation. She's bored, and Zahra still sometimes rejects them.
- **Nenek's counter-advice:** Her mother thinks she's over-complicating things; this undermines Siti's confidence.
- **No visibility into gaps:** She suspects Zahra isn't getting enough iron or protein on bad days but doesn't have a way to quantify this.
- **Recipe discovery:** Generic recipe sites show beautiful but impractical recipes. She needs simple, toddler-tested food.
- **Texture/preference tracking:** No good way to record what Zahra likes vs dislikes so the information isn't lost.

---

### Motivations

- Loves cooking and takes quiet pride in feeding her family well — picky eating dents that pride.
- Motivated by practical wins: one new accepted food is a big deal.
- Values community — wants to know other moms in her situation.
- Finds reassurance valuable: "okay overall, keep going" messages are meaningful to her.

---

### Behaviors

- Cooks 3 times a day; meals are central to her routine.
- Watches recipe videos on YouTube obsessively; often tries them only to have Zahra refuse them.
- Writes down Zahra's food wins in a notes app ("she ate brokoli today!!").
- Shares cooking results in a Bandung mom WhatsApp group with photos and captions.
- Uses Tokopedia for groceries when her husband is too busy to go to the market.

---

### Relationship to Suppa

- **Entry point:** WhatsApp group share from another Bandung mom, or a YouTube recipe channel mention.
- **Primary use case (M1):** Recipe discovery filtered by Zahra's dislikes; meal logging to see if "picky weeks" are nutritionally okay; saving family-tested recipes.
- **Key value:** The ability to mark Zahra's dislikes and get recipes that work around them — plus the reassurance module that shows cumulative patterns, not just today's "failure."
- **Risk:** If recipes feel too generic or Western, Siti won't trust them. Regional relevance (Sundanese/Indonesian food context) is important to her. The app should surface tempeh, tahu, bayam, kangkung — not salmon and quinoa.

### Research Interview Guide

**Goal:** Understand picky-eating coping strategies, recipe trust signals, and what reassurance looks like for a full-time caregiver.

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Describe Zahra's relationship with food. What are her current favorite meals?
- What does a "bad food day" look like for you and her?

**Core questions (20 min)**
1. When Zahra refuses a meal, what do you do? What goes through your mind afterward?
2. How do you currently decide what to try cooking for her? Where do you find ideas?
3. Have you ever felt confident Zahra was eating "well enough"? What did that feel like or look like?
4. What does Zahra's profile look like — what does she definitely eat, definitely avoid, and sometimes accept?
5. If an app could help you with Zahra's nutrition, what's the one thing it would need to understand about your situation?

**Usability probe (10 min)**
- Show the child profile / preferences screen. Ask: "How would you fill this in for Zahra? Does anything feel missing?"
- Show a recipe result card. Ask: "Would you trust this for Zahra? What makes you want to try it or skip it?"

**Wrap-up**
- What's your biggest unsolved question about Zahra's nutrition right now?
