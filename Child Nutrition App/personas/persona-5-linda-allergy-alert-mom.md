### Background

Dimas was diagnosed with a peanut allergy at 14 months following a mild reaction to a peanut-containing biscuit. The reaction required a clinic visit and was frightening enough that Linda has been vigilant ever since. She also suspects (but has no diagnosis for) a mild egg white sensitivity — Dimas's cheeks sometimes flush after certain meals. Her pediatric allergist has told her to monitor and reintroduce under supervision.

Living with her in-laws creates a constant food safety challenge. Her mertua cook traditional Javanese food daily — many dishes contain peanut-based sauces (bumbu kacang) or hidden egg. Linda has had multiple difficult conversations about this. She is not alarmist, but she is uncompromising on allergen safety. She does not trust verbal confirmation; she checks ingredients herself.

The nutrition side is secondary but real: because Dimas's diet is restricted, she worries about protein and fat variety — peanuts are a major protein source in Javanese food that she has to replace.

---

### Goals

1. Have a reliable system for flagging Dimas's allergens so any recipe or meal suggestion is automatically safe.
2. Manage the in-law conflict gracefully — ideally with a visible, shareable reference ("the app says no peanuts") that removes her as the authority.
3. Find protein-rich recipes that are peanut-safe and suitable for a 3-year-old who isn't a particularly adventurous eater.
4. Track and document any possible reactions for her allergist appointments.

---

### Pain Points

- **Hidden allergens:** Peanuts appear in many Indonesian dishes under different names (bumbu kacang, kacang tanah, selai kacang) — she has to stay vigilant even with home cooking.
- **In-law friction:** Her mertua occasionally forget or dismiss the allergy. Linda has to re-explain it repeatedly and feels like "the difficult one."
- **Suspected egg sensitivity:** Unconfirmed, but she logs it as a soft exclude and checks for patterns. She needs a way to record suspicions without treating them as hard rules.
- **Narrow safe recipe set:** With peanut excluded, a large portion of Javanese and general Indonesian recipe sites are unusable without modification.
- **No shared allergy reference:** She hasn't found a way to show the in-laws "the list" in a format they respect.

---

### Motivations

- Safety is non-negotiable — she would pay for an app that does allergen filtering correctly and doesn't make mistakes.
- Values documentation and receipts: logs reactions, notes ingredient decisions, and keeps records for medical visits.
- Wants to stop being the only person who holds Dimas's safety knowledge.
- Feels strongly about finding good Indonesian food alternatives — she doesn't want Dimas to grow up unable to enjoy local cuisine.

---

### Behaviors

- Reads ingredient lists on every packaged food before purchase.
- Maintains a personal WhatsApp note with "safe foods" and "unsafe foods" for Dimas.
- Uses Instagram to find recipe accounts; DMs recipe creators to ask about ingredients.
- Screenshots anything allergy-related from doctors and parenting sites.
- Coordinates with the in-laws daily about what Dimas can eat for lunch; still double-checks when she gets home.

---

### Relationship to Suppa

- **Entry point:** Allergy-focused parenting content on Instagram or a recommendation from her pediatric allergist's clinic.
- **Primary use case (M1):** Allergen profile setup; recipe filtering by allergen; meal logging with allergy-safe confirmation.
- **Key value:** The allergen hard-rule system is the core value proposition. If a recipe suggestion ever contains peanuts for Dimas, trust is immediately and permanently broken. Conversely, if the app consistently delivers safe suggestions, Linda becomes an evangelist.
- **Risk:** Any ambiguity in allergy matching (e.g. a recipe that uses "kacang" without specifying peanut vs other nuts) will flag her caution. The app must be explicit, never vague, about ingredient matching.
- **Secondary value:** A shareable, clean allergen profile she can show the in-laws or bring to the allergist.

### Research Interview Guide

**Goal:** Understand allergen vigilance, trust signals in recipe filtering, and the social/family dimension of managing a child's allergy in a multigenerational household.

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Tell me about Dimas and what happened when you discovered his allergy.
- What does daily food management look like in your household?

**Core questions (20 min)**
1. Walk me through how you decide whether a meal or recipe is safe for Dimas. What steps do you go through?
2. Have you ever had a close call or a mistake with Dimas's allergy? What happened?
3. How do you handle the in-laws and their cooking? What's worked, what hasn't?
4. What does "trustworthy" look like to you in an app that handles allergen filtering? What would immediately break that trust?
5. Is there anything about Dimas's nutrition beyond the peanut allergy that worries you?

**Usability probe (10 min)**
- Show the child allergy profile screen. Ask: "Set up Dimas's allergens. Is anything missing or unclear?"
- Show a recipe result filtered for Dimas. Ask: "How confident are you that this is safe? What would increase your confidence?"

**Wrap-up**
- If you found an app that handled Dimas's allergy correctly every time — what else would need to be true for you to use it daily?
